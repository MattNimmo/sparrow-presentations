Code of Conduct

All roles

As a Sparrow Moving team member, you represent our company every time you interact with customers, vendors, and the public. This code of conduct outlines the behavior standards that help us maintain our reputation for excellence.

Core Values

Everything we do at Sparrow flows from three core values. Every decision you make on the job should reflect them:

  • Hospitality: We want to create hospitable environments in every space we step into. That looks like warmth, generosity, care, compassion, and friendliness — in every home, for every customer.
  • Integrity: Trust and accountability matter in every interaction. Our commitment is to provide clear, consistent, and honest communication — always.
  • Honor: We celebrate our team and strive to create a culture of growth and teamwork where everyone is taken care of.

Customer Interaction

First Impressions

You only get one chance to make a first impression. When arriving at a job:

  • Introduce yourself and your crew by name
  • Shake hands (if offered) and make eye contact
  • Explain what to expect and the general timeline
  • Ask about any special concerns or items requiring extra care

Communication Standards

  • Use professional language at all times—no profanity
  • Listen actively when customers speak
  • Answer questions honestly; if you don't know, say "Let me find out for you"
  • Keep customers informed of progress and any delays
  • Thank customers at the end of every job
The 10/5 Rule: At 10 feet, make eye contact and smile. At 5 feet, greet the person verbally. This applies to customers and their neighbors.

Customer Property

  • Treat every item as if it were your own grandmother's heirloom
  • Ask before using customer bathrooms, and leave them cleaner than you found them
  • Never eat or drink in customer homes unless offered
  • Never smoke on customer property

Handling Complaints

If a customer expresses dissatisfaction:

  1. Listen without interrupting
  2. Apologize for their frustration (not necessarily for fault)
  3. Ask what you can do to make it right
  4. Involve your crew lead or dispatch if needed
  5. Never argue with a customer

Team Conduct

Working with Your Crew

  • Communicate clearly about what you're doing and where you need help
  • Pull your weight—don't leave the heavy lifting to others
  • Offer help when you see a teammate struggling
  • Save personal conversations for breaks, not in front of customers
  • Address conflicts privately and professionally

Prohibited Behavior

The following behaviors are grounds for disciplinary action or termination:

  • Harassment of any kind
  • Discrimination based on any protected characteristic
  • Violence or threats of violence
  • Theft or dishonesty
  • Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol
  • Insubordination or refusal to follow reasonable instructions
  • Sharing customer information without authorization

Uniform & Appearance

Required Uniform

  • Shirt: Clean Sparrow Moving branded shirt (provided)
  • Pants: Dark-colored work pants or shorts (no jeans with holes)

Grooming Standards

  • Arrive clean and showered
  • Hair should be neat and secured if long (for safety)
  • Facial hair should be groomed
  • Avoid strong fragrances (cologne, perfume)
  • Keep fingernails trimmed for safety and professionalism

Not Permitted

  • Visible offensive tattoos (must be covered)
  • Clothing with offensive graphics or language
  • Dangling jewelry that could catch on items
  • Open-toed shoes or sandals

Social Media Policy

Representing Sparrow Online

  • Do not post photos of customer homes, belongings, or any identifying information
  • Do not post negative comments about customers, even anonymously
  • Do not share internal company information publicly
  • If posting about work, be positive and professional

Personal Social Media

Your personal accounts are yours, but remember that you may be associated with Sparrow. Avoid posts that could reflect poorly on the company or your professionalism.

Privacy is Paramount: Posting customer photos, addresses, or any details about their moves is a serious violation and grounds for immediate termination.

Reporting Concerns

If you witness behavior that violates this code of conduct, report it to:

  • Your crew lead (for immediate on-site issues)
  • Your supervisor or the office (for ongoing concerns)
  • Anonymously via our reporting system (details in office)

All reports are taken seriously and investigated. Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is prohibited.
