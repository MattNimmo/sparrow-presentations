Employee Handbook

All roles
Welcome to Sparrow Moving! This handbook contains everything you need to know about working with us. Keep it handy and refer back whenever you have questions.

About Sparrow Moving

Matt & Mark Nimmo had some friends and family members who had less than awesome moving experiences. So we decided to not point out a problem but bring a solution, Sparrow was born.

Our Mission

"Sparrow moves families forward, caring for every box and piece of furniture as if it were our own, bringing a new level of hospitality into the moving industry."

Our Core Values

  • Hospitality: We want to create hospitable environments in every space we step into. That looks like warmth, generosity, care, compassion, and friendliness — in every home, for every client.
  • Integrity: Trust and accountability matter in every interaction. We commit to clear, consistent, and honest communication — always.
  • Honor: We celebrate our team and strive to create a culture of growth and teamwork where everyone is taken care of.

Employment Relationship

At-Will Employment

Employment with Sparrow Moving is at-will, meaning either you or the company may end the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause or notice. This handbook does not constitute an employment contract.

Pay & Compensation

Pay Schedule

All employees are paid weekly on Fridays. If a payday falls on a holiday, you will be paid on the preceding business day.

Overtime

Non-exempt employees earn overtime pay at 1.5x their regular rate for all hours worked over 40 in a workweek. Overtime must be approved by your supervisor in advance when possible.

Role-Specific Compensation

For detailed pay structures and bonus criteria specific to your role, see the Compensation documents.

Time Off & Leave

Paid Time Off (PTO)

Employees accrue PTO based on hours worked:

  • Accrual rate: 1 hour of PTO per 30 hours worked
  • Annual cap: Up to 48 hours (6 days) per year
  • Carryover: Up to 48 hours may carry into the next calendar year

PTO requests must be submitted at least 2 weeks in advance for planned time off. Requests during peak moving season (May–September) may be limited based on business need.

Sick Leave

In compliance with the Minnesota Earned Sick and Safe Time (ESST) Act, all employees accrue paid sick leave at 1 hour per 30 hours worked, up to 48 hours per year. Up to 48 hours may carry over into the next year.

Sick leave may be used for:

  • Your own illness, injury, or medical or mental health care
  • Care for a family member's illness, injury, or medical appointment
  • Situations involving domestic violence, sexual assault, or stalking (for you or a family member)
  • Closure of your workplace or a family member's school/care facility due to a public health emergency

Holidays

Sparrow Moving observes the following paid holidays:

  • Thanksgiving Day
  • Christmas Day

Team Conduct

Working with Your Team

  • Treat others the way you want to be treated
  • We treat our teammates with respect
  • Insults and name calling are not tolerated
  • Communicate clearly about what you're doing and where you need help
  • Pull your weight — don't leave the heavy lifting to others
  • Offer help when you see a teammate struggling
  • Save personal conversations for breaks, not in front of clients
  • Problems are dealt with team leads — we don't argue with the client

On the Job

  • We don't smoke on the job
  • We don't cuss around clients
  • We don't talk religion or politics with our clients

Anti-Harassment

Sparrow Moving is committed to a workplace free from harassment. The following are prohibited and grounds for disciplinary action or termination:

  • Harassment of any kind — verbal (slurs, jokes, comments), physical (unwanted touching), or visual (offensive images, gestures)
  • Sexual harassment of any kind
  • Discrimination based on any protected characteristic
  • Violence or threats of violence
  • Theft or dishonesty
  • Being under the influence of drugs or alcohol
  • Refusing to follow reasonable instructions from your team lead
  • Sharing client information without authorization

If you experience or witness harassment, report it immediately to your team lead or the office. All reports will be investigated promptly and confidentially.

Uniform & Appearance

Required Uniform

  • Shirt: Official Sparrow Moving t-shirt or polo — must be clean and presentable. You receive one shirt upon hire and a second shirt at 30 days. Additional shirts can be purchased for $30.
  • Pants: Athletic wear is acceptable year-round — athletic shorts or joggers are fine in both summer and winter. Clothing must be clean and free of offensive graphics.
  • Belt: Required if pants are too large or sag.
  • Footwear: Tennis shoes with non-slip soles — required for safety. No open-toed shoes or sandals.

Grooming Standards

  • Arrive clean and showered
  • Hair should be neat and secured if long (for safety)
  • Facial hair should be groomed
  • Avoid strong fragrances (cologne, perfume)
  • Keep fingernails trimmed for safety and professionalism

Not Permitted

  • Visible offensive tattoos (must be covered)
  • Clothing with offensive graphics or language
  • Dangling jewelry that could catch on items

Social Media Policy

Representing Sparrow Online

  • Do not post photos of client homes, belongings, or any identifying information
  • Do not post negative comments about clients, even anonymously
  • Do not share internal company information publicly
  • If posting about work, be positive and professional

Personal Social Media

Your personal accounts are yours, but remember that you may be associated with Sparrow. Avoid posts that could reflect poorly on the company or your professionalism.

Privacy is Paramount: Posting client photos, addresses, or any details about their moves is a serious violation and grounds for immediate termination.

Client Interaction

Handling Complaints

If a client expresses dissatisfaction:

  1. Never argue with the client
  2. Listen without interrupting
  3. Apologize for their frustration (not necessarily for fault)
  4. Involve your team lead or dispatch if needed

Client Property

  • Treat every item as if it were your own grandmother's heirloom
  • Ask before using client bathrooms, and leave them cleaner than you found them
  • Never eat or drink in client homes unless offered
  • Never smoke on client property

Attendance & Punctuality

Timeliness is critical to operational efficiency and client satisfaction.

  • Arrive on time for your scheduled shift
  • If you anticipate being late, notify your team lead immediately
  • Provide as much advance notice as possible for planned absences
  • Unexcused absences (no call/no show) will result in immediate suspension and potential termination
No Call, No Show: Failing to show up for a scheduled shift without notification is grounds for immediate termination.

Breaks

  • Employees are entitled to a maximum of three five-minute breaks during a 4-hour shift, or one 15-minute break. Breaks must be coordinated with the team lead.
  • Lunch breaks are unpaid if taken off-site. If taken at the client's location, employees remain on the clock with prior approval from the team lead.

Gifts & Tipping

  • Employees may accept tips if offered voluntarily by clients. Tips must never be solicited. If a client asks, respond: "Tips are never expected but always appreciated."
  • All cash tips must be reported in the daily feedback notes. Tips will be distributed as part of the team's paycheck or divided among the team at the end of the shift (off the clock).
  • If a client offers a gift (furniture or other items), approval from the team lead is required. A gifting form must be completed, and the item must be taken immediately following the shift.

Workplace Policies

Drug & Alcohol Policy

Sparrow Moving maintains a drug-free workplace. The use, possession, sale, or being under the influence of illegal drugs or alcohol while on duty is strictly prohibited and grounds for immediate termination.

Employees in safety-sensitive positions may be subject to random drug testing as required by DOT regulations.

Disciplinary Process

Sparrow Moving uses a progressive discipline approach for most policy violations:

  1. Verbal Warning: Documented conversation about the issue
  2. Written Warning: Formal documentation with improvement plan
  3. Final Warning: Last chance with clear consequences
  4. Termination: If improvement is not made

Serious violations (theft, violence, harassment, no-call no-show, etc.) may result in immediate termination without progressive discipline.

Strike System

Certain infractions are tracked as strikes. Accumulating 3 strikes within a 60-day period is grounds for disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

The following each result in a strike:

  • Being late without communicating — if you're going to be late, you must notify your team lead in advance. Arriving late with no communication is an automatic strike. Whether a late arrival that was communicated results in a strike is at the team lead's discretion.
  • Coming in smelling like pot or cigarettes
  • Unapproved absence — notifying us with less than 48 hours notice that you're unavailable

Zero-Tolerance Policy on Theft

Any theft, misappropriation, or destruction of company or client property will result in immediate termination and possible legal action.

Resignation & Termination

Voluntary Resignation

If you choose to leave Sparrow Moving, we request at least 2 weeks written notice. This allows us to transition your responsibilities and ensures you leave on good terms for future references.

Final Paycheck

Upon separation, you will receive your final paycheck on the next regular payday.

Return of Property

All company property (uniforms, equipment, keys, etc.) must be returned on your last day of work.

Reporting Concerns

If you witness behavior that violates this handbook, report it to:

  • Your team lead (for immediate on-site issues)
  • Your supervisor or the office (for ongoing concerns)
  • Anonymously via our reporting system (details in office)

All reports are taken seriously and investigated. Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern in good faith is prohibited.
