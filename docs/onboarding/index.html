Onboarding Checklist

All roles
Welcome to the team! This checklist will guide you through your first days at Sparrow Moving. Complete each section in order.

Before Your First Day

Complete these items before arriving for your first shift:

☐
Complete employment paperwork

W-4, I-9, direct deposit form, emergency contact

☐
Provide valid ID documents

Driver's license + Social Security card (or passport)

☐
Complete background check authorization

Required for all positions

☐
Complete drug test

Required prior to first shift

Your 3-Week On-Ramp

Sparrow uses a structured 3-week training program. You'll be paired with a mentor — a seasoned crew leader or veteran teammate — for your first 2–4 weeks. They'll demonstrate every skill, coach you in the moment, and sign off on your certification.

Week 1 — Orientation & Foundations

☐
Review company values, policies & procedures

Hospitality, Integrity, Honor — know what they mean on the job

☐
Review the Moving Essentials handbook

Safety manual, training SOPs, code of conduct

☐
Shadow mentor on real jobs — light tasks only

Light boxes and floor runners to start; observe everything else

Week 2 — Hands-On Independence

☐
Take the lead on simple tasks

E.g. disassembling a bed frame, wrapping dressers

☐
Plan and load part of a small move under mentor guidance

Weight distribution, heavy items first, securing the load

☐
Mid-week check-in with mentor

Fine-tune skills; identify any gaps before Week 3

Week 3 — Targeted Skill Building & Test-Out Prep

☐
Identify and close remaining skill gaps

Appliance prep, inventory paperwork, pre-trip checks

☐
Practice with job-specific how-to guides

Review Training & SOPs and complete on-site demos

☐
Complete your final test-out and certification

See Test-Out section below

Test-Out: Core Skills Certification

After approximately 20 moves, your mentor will observe you perform each of the following skills without prompting. Pass all five and you're certified as a full Sparrow teammate.

Rules: You must complete each skill without being prompted. Safety-critical coaching is allowed, but it counts against you. Your mentor signs off upon completion.
☐
Safe lifting technique
☐
Padding & wrapping furniture
☐
Floor & doorway protection
☐
Dolly & hand truck operation
☐
Truck loading basics

90-Day Review

At 90 days, you'll have a formal performance review:

  • Evaluation of job performance and reliability
  • Customer feedback review
  • Safety record check
  • Discussion of pay grade advancement (if applicable)
  • Goal setting for next quarter
Probation period: Your first 90 days are considered a probationary period. Successful completion confirms your position and may qualify you for a pay increase.

Your Mentor

Your mentor will demonstrate every essential skill, let you try tasks yourself once you've seen them done, coach you in the moment when technique slips, and debrief with you at the end of each shift.

Don't hesitate to ask your mentor anything — they've been in your shoes and they're here to make you great.
