Injury & Workers' Comp

All roles
Emergency? For serious injuries, call 911 first. Then notify dispatch at (612) 444-1284.

Report ALL Injuries

Every injury must be reported, no matter how minor it seems. This protects you and ensures you receive proper care if the injury worsens later.

Why report minor injuries?

  • A small strain can become a serious problem if untreated
  • Workers' comp coverage requires timely reporting
  • Documentation protects your rights
  • Helps us identify and fix safety hazards

Immediate Response Steps

Step 1: Stop Work

If you're injured, stop what you're doing immediately. Don't try to "push through" an injury.

Step 2: Assess the Situation

  • Life-threatening or severe? Call 911 immediately
  • Moderate injury? Seek medical attention promptly
  • Minor injury? Administer first aid, continue monitoring

Step 3: Notify Your Crew Lead

Tell your crew lead immediately, even for minor injuries. They will:

  • Ensure you receive appropriate care
  • Document the incident
  • Contact dispatch
  • Arrange coverage if you need to leave the job

Step 4: Complete Incident Report

An incident report must be completed within 24 hours. Your crew lead or the office will provide the form. Include:

  • Date, time, and location of injury
  • Description of what happened
  • Body part(s) affected
  • Witnesses present
  • Treatment received

Seeking Medical Treatment

Where to Go

  • Emergency: Nearest emergency room or call 911
  • Urgent but not emergency: Urgent care clinic
  • Non-urgent: Your primary care doctor or workers' comp approved provider
Important: Tell the medical provider that this is a work-related injury. This ensures proper billing to workers' compensation.

What to Tell the Doctor

  • This injury happened at work
  • Your employer is Sparrow Moving
  • Exactly how the injury occurred
  • All symptoms, even minor ones

Medical Documentation

Keep copies of:

  • Doctor's notes and diagnoses
  • Treatment plans
  • Work restrictions or accommodations
  • Prescriptions
  • Follow-up appointment schedules

Workers' Compensation

What It Covers

Workers' comp provides benefits for work-related injuries:

  • Medical expenses: Doctor visits, hospital stays, medications, physical therapy
  • Lost wages: Partial wage replacement if you can't work
  • Rehabilitation: Physical therapy and vocational rehab if needed
  • Disability benefits: For permanent impairment, if applicable

Your Rights

  • You have the right to file a workers' comp claim
  • You cannot be fired for filing a claim
  • You have the right to medical treatment
  • You have the right to return to work when medically cleared

Filing a Claim

The office handles most of the paperwork, but you need to:

  1. Report the injury immediately
  2. Complete the incident report
  3. Provide medical documentation
  4. Follow up with the office on claim status

Return to Work

Light Duty

If the doctor clears you for light duty, we may have modified work available:

  • Office or warehouse tasks
  • Inventory and organization
  • Training assistance
  • Equipment maintenance

Full Return

To return to full duty, you must:

  • Provide a written release from your doctor
  • Have no restrictions that affect job performance
  • Complete a return-to-work meeting with your supervisor

Restrictions & Accommodations

If you have permanent restrictions, talk to the office about accommodations. We'll work with you to find solutions that keep you employed safely.

Preventing Future Injuries

After any injury, we conduct a review to prevent recurrence:

  • What caused the injury?
  • Could it have been prevented?
  • What changes should we make?
  • Does anyone need additional training?

Your input is valuable—help us make the job safer for everyone.
