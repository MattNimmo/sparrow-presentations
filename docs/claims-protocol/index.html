Property Damage & Claims

Mover Crew lead

Damage happens, even to the best crews. How you handle it makes all the difference. Follow this protocol to protect the customer, the company, and yourself.

Golden Rule: Never hide damage. Honesty builds trust and protects everyone. Hidden damage discovered later is much worse than damage reported immediately.

When Damage Occurs

Step 1: Stop and Assess

  • Stop work immediately
  • Make sure no one is injured
  • Assess the extent of the damage
  • Do not attempt to repair anything yourself

Step 2: Notify Your Crew Lead

If you're not the crew lead, inform them immediately. The crew lead is responsible for managing the situation from here.

Step 3: Document with Photos

Take clear photos immediately:

  • Wide shot: Shows the item and surrounding area
  • Close-up: Shows the damage in detail
  • Multiple angles: At least 3-4 photos of each damage
  • Context: Photos showing how/where damage occurred
Photo tips: Good lighting, steady hand, include something for scale if helpful. Time-stamped photos are best—your phone does this automatically.

Step 4: Inform the Customer

The crew lead should:

  • Approach the customer calmly and professionally
  • Explain what happened honestly
  • Apologize for the inconvenience
  • Assure them it will be handled through proper channels
  • Do NOT discuss payment or compensation

What to Say

"I want to let you know that [item] was damaged during the move. I've documented it with photos and will report it to our office immediately. They'll be in touch with you to resolve this. I apologize for the inconvenience."

What NOT to Say

  • "It was like that before" (unless you have pre-move photos proving it)
  • "I'll pay for it myself"
  • "It's not that bad"
  • "We're not responsible for that"
  • Any specific dollar amounts

Step 5: Contact Dispatch

Call dispatch immediately to report the damage. Provide:

  • Customer name and job number
  • Description of damaged item
  • How damage occurred
  • Customer's reaction
  • Confirmation that photos were taken

Pre-Existing Damage

Protect yourself by documenting pre-existing damage BEFORE you touch anything:

During Walk-Through

  • Point out any existing damage to the customer
  • Take photos of items that already have damage
  • Note pre-existing damage on the inventory sheet
  • Have customer initial acknowledgment if possible

Red Flag Items

Pay extra attention to items prone to existing damage:

  • Glass and mirrors (look for chips, cracks)
  • Wood furniture (scratches, water rings, veneer issues)
  • Antiques and older items
  • Appliances (dents, scratches)
  • Electronics (screens, corners)
  • Upholstered items (stains, tears)

Types of Damage

Customer Property

Furniture, boxes, belongings. Follow the full protocol above.

Customer Home

Walls, floors, door frames, etc. Same protocol, plus:

  • Stop using that path if possible
  • Add additional protection to prevent further damage
  • Photos should include existing room condition

Company Property

Truck, equipment, supplies. Report to crew lead and dispatch. Complete equipment damage report.

Claims Process

What Happens Next

  1. Office reviews damage report and photos
  2. Office contacts customer within 24-48 hours
  3. Claim is evaluated based on coverage and circumstances
  4. Resolution is offered to customer (repair, replacement, or compensation)

Your Role

  • Cooperate with any investigation
  • Provide additional information if requested
  • Do NOT contact the customer about the claim
  • Do NOT discuss the claim with anyone outside the company

Consequences of Damage

Accidents Happen

Occasional accidents are part of the job. You won't be penalized for honest mistakes if you:

  • Report damage immediately
  • Follow proper protocol
  • Were using proper techniques
  • Learn from the incident

Patterns Are a Problem

Repeated damage incidents may result in:

  • Additional training requirements
  • Increased supervision
  • Disciplinary action
  • Pay deductions for negligent damage

Negligence

Damage caused by clear negligence (horseplay, ignoring safety procedures, rushing recklessly) may result in:

  • Written warning or termination
  • Loss of bonuses

Prevention

The best claim is the one that never happens:

  • Take your time—rushing causes damage
  • Use proper wrapping and padding
  • Protect floors and door frames
  • Communicate with your team
  • Ask for help with heavy/awkward items
  • Use equipment properly
  • Stay focused—distractions lead to accidents
