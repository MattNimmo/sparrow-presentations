Pack & Prep

Mover Crew lead

Proper packing and preparation prevents damage, builds customer trust, and keeps the job moving efficiently. This page covers every technique Sparrow movers use — materials, box types, labeling, home protection, lifting, and room-by-room specifics.

Pro Tip: Take an extra 10 minutes to prep properly and you'll save 30 minutes on the job — plus avoid damage claims.

Box Types

Bigger box = lighter contents. Max 50 lbs per box.

Telescoping: Two wardrobe boxes nested together create a custom-length extended box. Count as two cartons on the customer inventory.

Building & Packing Boxes

  • Set up a packing station on a flip-top table or flat carton — never on customer furniture
  • Build box: fold flaps, pop open, tape bottom with 3 strips (1 center + 1 each side)
  • Tape tear tip: brace both fingers against the roll for a cleaner, faster rip
  • Pack heaviest items on bottom, lightest on top; balance weight side to side
  • Box should be full but not bulging — if top flaps sink, stuff with more paper
  • Custom half-height box: Cut corners of a book box ~⅔ down, fold walls inward, tape. Use for heavy/compact items like dumbbells

Labeling System

Label the side, not the top. The top gets covered when boxes are stacked. Draw an arrow on top pointing toward the labeled side.

Every label includes:

  • Room abbreviation: MBR (master bedroom), KIT (kitchen), LVR (living room), etc.
  • Contents: Specific items, not just the room name — e.g., "pots, pans, utensils"
  • Special handling in ALL CAPS: FRAGILE, HEAVY, TOPLOAD ONLY
  • Packer initials
  • PBO = packed by owner  |  CP = company packed

Packing Materials

  • Newsprint (packing paper): Line every box bottom. Wrap delicate items. Fill all gaps. Be generous.
  • Shrink wrap: Keeps pads in place, bundles loose parts, surface protection. Wrap with tension but don't compress. Never use directly on leather or vinyl — traps moisture, causes damage.
  • Mattress bags: Every mattress and box spring, no exceptions.
  • Packing tape: For sealing boxes only — never tape directly to furniture surfaces
  • Painter's tape: Labeling and temporary holds on surfaces

Protecting the Home

Floors

  • Lay neoprene floor runners (red) from door to truck path — absorbent and protective
  • Lay cardboard under the ramp
  • Movers cannot remove shoes (insurance requirement) — runners are the solution

Stairs

  • Use stair runner
  • Tape to carpet only — never tape to finished wood (heat softens adhesive and damages the finish)
  • Use a slice of mirror box as a landing pad at the base of the stairs

Door Jambs & Railings

  • Install soft protectors on door jambs, secured with painter's tape
  • Edges take more hits than center panels — prioritize corners
  • Wrap railings in moving pads; open/close to reposition rather than dragging

Pre-Existing Damage

Document before work begins. Photograph any existing damage and text your crew lead. Upload to CRM. Take photos with crew visibly prepping in the background to timestamp that work hadn't started.

Lifting Techniques

The Coffin Carry

For large flat furniture (dressers, tabletops):

  1. Two movers, one on each side
  2. Tilt piece backward onto rear legs at ~30°
  3. Both movers reach under and place hands under front feet (now off the ground)
  4. Lift together; level the piece out
  5. Shift the hand that supported the back top edge to support the rear feet

The 45 — For Stairs

  1. Person at top grips the bottom edge; person at bottom supports the higher end
  2. Top person controls the tilt and calls out: "pressing up," "clear below," "going tall"
  3. In tight stairwells: may need to go fully vertical, twisted around corners

Dolly Rules

  • Level ground: Pull dolly behind you
  • Outdoors / down steps: Dolly in front — you are the brake
  • Up a ramp: Your body goes first, dolly follows
  • Down a ramp: Dolly leads, you control the descent
  • Heavy items always need a second person on the dolly — one grips, one guides from the other side
  • Never stack above the dolly handle

Hump Strap Uses

  • King mattresses: Loop around wrists, mirror image of partner, scoop the long edge
  • Stack of boxes: Lay across surface, stack boxes, swing over shoulder, pull from below and over the same shoulder
  • Wardrobe boxes: Swing over head, squat, pull tight — box cradles on upper back

Verbal Cues

Room-by-Room

Bedroom

  • Mattresses: Slide into bag from top, tip on side, fold excess plastic, tape shut
  • Specialty beds (Sleep Number, Purple, Tempurpedic): Flag during estimate — require third-party specialist
  • Bed frames: All hardware in labeled parts bag. Remove spindles before banding (pressure can crack them). Wrap headboard, footboard, side rails, and platform slats individually
  • Dressers: Remove attached mirror first (partner stabilizes while you unscrew). Note any mismatched or stripped hardware in inventory
  • Mirrors: Lay pad on floor in diamond shape, set mirror diagonally, fold all four corners over glass, band snugly
  • Clothes in drawers: Soft items (t-shirts, linens) can stay. Hard or fragile items: remove. Going to storage: box everything
  • Wardrobe boxes: Transfer hanging clothes directly from closet. Use the bottom space for shoes, bags, and light items

Dining Room

  • Table: Remove glass top first. Flip upside down. Remove legs — reattach screws to legs or use labeled parts bag. Wrap, figure-eight twist banding at ends, one final band across the face
  • Chairs: Pad diagonally — fold front corner in, then side corners, then tuck rear corner through the base
  • Captain's chairs (with arms): Use 2 pads for full coverage
  • Bench: Two pads diagonally overlapping, wrap legs with bands

Kitchen

Dish pack 3-tier system:

  • Bottom tier: Heavy plates and bowls on their edge, never flat. Wrap-build method: plate → fold left → plate → fold right → fold top/bottom → wrap bundle
  • Middle tier: Mugs and glassware. Add paper cushion layer between tiers
  • Top tier: Light plastics, utensils. Champagne flutes go in a separate book box
  • Always finish with crumpled paper on top to prevent collapse from stacking
  • Silver: Handle with paper — skin oils cause tarnish. Wrap in tissue then newsprint
  • Knife block: 3 layers of wrap, fully taped, labeled "KNIFE BLOCK — BLADES INSIDE," set sideways with label facing up
  • Loose knives: Wrap blade side thick, tape closed, mark which end has the point. Label "BLADES"
  • Glass bowls: Wrap individually, nest upside down (contents sift downward for protection)
  • Pots/pans: Use 4.5 or 3 cube box — not a dish pack. Nest with paper between; flip lids upside down inside pot
  • Canned goods: Book box only — dense and heavy. Group by cabinet
  • Glass jars: Wrap individually, upright, fill all voids — if it rattles, it'll break

Living Room — Items

  • Books/CDs/DVDs: Book box, upright. Rare or sentimental books: wrap in newsprint
  • Picture frames: One at a time on newsprint, wrap in layers, pack bundle vertically — never flat
  • Lamps: Remove shade, harp, and finial (keep together with the shade). Unscrew bulb — it goes with the base, not the shade (wrong bulb + wrong lamp = overheating). Wrap base upright; up to 5 bases per box (corners + center). Shade in a separate box with crumpled paper interior support
  • Cords: Bundle together; maintain the customer's organization system if they had one
  • Live plants: Cannot be moved — state regulations. Customer must transport themselves
  • Electronics: Ask if the customer has original packaging before boxing

Living Room — Furniture

  • Couch: Remove all cushions (wardrobe box). Remove sectional backs if detachable. Tuck power cords into back pad before wrapping. Use shrink wrap or pad depending on upholstery — check with crew lead. Coffin carry out
  • Recliners: Break down back if possible. Lay pad at 45°, fold four corners in, band. Drape pad over seat, band vertically and horizontally. Tip on side, shrink wrap top-to-bottom and side-to-side
  • Side tables with glass: Wrap glass separately (2 pads + tape, or mirror carton). Wrap table base in pad, fold corners in, band
  • Wall art / large clocks: Build mirror carton with minimal tape. Line interior with crumpled paper — do not wrap artwork directly. Insert art, add light corner padding, tape all seams. Label: e.g., "LVRM mantle art"

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Taping directly to wood finishes or furniture surfaces
  • Using stretch wrap on leather or vinyl
  • Labeling the top of boxes instead of the side
  • Overpacking boxes — if it bulges, redistribute
  • Leaving hardware loose — always bag and tape to the item
  • Taping stair runners to finished wood floors
  • Skipping pre-existing damage documentation
  • Rushing the prep phase
