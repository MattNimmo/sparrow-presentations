Vehicle Operation Policy

Crew lead

As a crew lead, you're responsible for the safe operation of Sparrow vehicles. This policy covers DOT compliance, inspections, and accident procedures.

Driver Requirements

To Drive Sparrow Vehicles, You Must:

  • Be at least 21 years old
  • Hold a valid driver's license (appropriate class for vehicle)
  • Be approved through Sparrow's driver authorization process
  • Have a clean driving record (no major violations in past 3 years)
  • Complete driver training with a supervisor
  • Pass a DOT physical (if required for vehicle class)

License Requirements by Vehicle

Cargo vans & pickup trucks Standard license
16-ft box trucks Standard license
26-ft box trucks Standard license

Pre-Trip Inspection

Complete a pre-trip inspection BEFORE every shift. This is required by DOT and protects you, your crew, and others on the road.

Pre-Trip Checklist

Walk-Around Inspection:

☐ Tires (pressure, tread, damage)
☐ Lights (headlights, brake lights, turn signals, markers)
☐ Mirrors (adjusted, clean, not cracked)
☐ Windshield (no cracks, wipers working)
☐ Fluid levels (oil, coolant, washer fluid)
☐ Brakes (test before leaving lot)
☐ Horn (working)
☐ Cargo area (clean, secure, ramp working)
☐ Safety equipment (fire extinguisher, triangles, first aid)

Documenting Issues

If you find any issues:

  • Minor issues: Note on inspection form, report to dispatch, proceed if safe
  • Major issues: Do NOT drive the vehicle. Report immediately for repair or replacement

Safe Driving Practices

General Rules

  • Obey all traffic laws—no exceptions
  • No cell phone use while driving (hands-free calls for dispatch only)
  • Always wear seatbelt
  • No passengers except authorized employees
  • No eating while driving
  • Allow extra stopping distance for loaded trucks
  • Check mirrors frequently

Speed Limits

  • Never exceed posted limits
  • Reduce speed in bad weather, heavy traffic, or unfamiliar areas
  • Remember: loaded trucks handle differently than empty ones

Backing Up

GOAL: Get Out And Look. Before backing, exit the cab and check behind the vehicle. Use a spotter whenever possible.
  • Always use a spotter when available
  • If no spotter, get out and look before backing
  • Back slowly and check mirrors constantly
  • If you can't see, stop and re-evaluate

Clearance

  • Know your vehicle's height (posted in cab)
  • Check clearance BEFORE entering any structure, bridge, or drive-through
  • When in doubt, find an alternate route
  • Low-clearance damage is 100% preventable

Accident Procedures

At the Scene

  1. Stop immediately—never leave the scene
  2. Check for injuries—call 911 if anyone is hurt
  3. Move to safety if possible (out of traffic)
  4. Turn on hazard lights and set out triangles if safe
  5. Call dispatch immediately
  6. Exchange information with other parties (name, insurance, license plate)
  7. Document everything—photos of all vehicles, damage, scene, road conditions
  8. Get witness information if available
  9. Wait for police—get a copy of the report number

What NOT to Do

  • Do NOT admit fault or apologize
  • Do NOT discuss the accident with anyone except police and dispatch
  • Do NOT sign anything except the police report
  • Do NOT leave the scene until released by police

After the Accident

  • Complete accident report form
  • Submit all photos and documentation to office
  • You may be required to take a post-accident drug test
  • Cooperate fully with any investigation

Fuel & Maintenance

Fueling

  • Team Lead uses company card for all fuel purchases
  • Diesel trucks = DIESEL ONLY (green handle)
  • Gasoline trucks = GASOLINE ONLY
  • Misfueling is expensive and 100% preventable
  • Keep tank at least 1/4 full

Reporting Issues

Report any mechanical issues immediately:

  • Strange noises, vibrations, or smells
  • Warning lights
  • Brake issues
  • Steering problems
  • Any safety concern

Long Distance DOT Regulations

When operating across state lines or on long-distance moves, additional federal DOT regulations apply. These rules exist to keep drivers alert and roads safe.

Additional Requirements

  • Valid DOT medical card may be required (renewed every 2 years)
  • Pre-trip and post-trip inspection documentation required
  • Electronic Logging Device (ELD) required for Hours of Service tracking on applicable trips

Hours of Service (HOS)

  • 11-hour driving limit after 10 consecutive hours off duty
  • 14-hour window after coming on duty
  • 30-minute break required after 8 hours of driving
  • 60/70-hour limits over 7/8 consecutive days
HOS Violations: Falsifying logs or violating Hours of Service rules can result in termination.
